    • Q1. To act as a PIC of an aircraft, what kind of documents do pilot must possess?


      • Pilot certificate Medical certificate Government-issued photo id


    • Q1-1. What are the requirements to be a private pilot?


      • At least student pilot (PPL) At least private pilot (CPL) Fluent English skill Enough aeronautical experience Pass written, practical test posses medical certification and ID


    • Q1-2. What are the privileges of a private pilot?


      • you may act as a PIC of an aircraft with no compensation and pro-rata share of operational cost. you may fly through class B you may act as a PIC of an aircraft for compensation only when it is incidental to the business you may act as a PIC of an aircraft for the purpose of nonprofit and charitable. to demonstrate an aircraft to the potential buyer when you have logged more than 200 hours. to search and rescue purposes.


    • Q1-2-1. What are the privileges of a commercial pilot?


      • The pilot can act as a PIC of an aircraft for compensation or hire. The pilot can act as a PIC of an aircraft carrying passengers or cargo for compensation for hire.


    • Q1-2-2. What are the limitations of a commercial pilot?


      • If the pilot have an instrument rating, there is no limitation. Commercial pilot without an instrument rating will not be allowed to carry passengers for hire on CX flight in excess of 50NM or night is prohibited


    • Q1-3. Does a pilot license expire?


      • No, the pilot license does not expire. However, the pilots have to operate a review flight every 24 calendar months with an authorized instructor. Review flight consists of a minimum of 1hour of flight training and 1 hour of ground training. Further details can be found in FAR 61.56


    • Q1-4. What does pilot need to do for carrying a passenger as a commercial pilot?


      • The pilot must operate at least 3 take-offs and landings within the preceding 90days. If it is a night flight, take-off and landing must be full stop.


    • Q2. Explain types of medical certificates.


      • There are three types of medical certificate, first, second, and third. The pilot can act as a PIC in an airline with a first medical certificate. The pilot can act as a PIC as a commercial pilot with a second medical certificate. The pilot can act as a student pilot, private pilot, or instructor with a third medical certificate.


    • Q2-1. Then, how often does a pilot need to renew their medical certificate?


      • If a pilot age below 40, the pilot needs to renew their medical certificate every 12, 12, 60 calendar months respectively. If a pilot age at or above 40, the pilot needs to renew their medical certificate every 6, 12, 24 calendar months respectively.


    • Q2-2. If you had the first medical certificate a year ago, now do you have to renew your medical certificate?


      • To act as a PIC of an airline pilot, yes I have to. However, since I am a private pilot(student or CFI), I still have 48months of third medical certificate privilege.
